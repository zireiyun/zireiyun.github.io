In Retrospect

Published November 6, 2021

It started off with a selection interview, then an orientation lunch, followed by 3 full days of monthly classes over the course of 7 months, from March to September. It ended (for now) with writing and presenting a plan. 3 more months of mentoring and learning group session to go.

Here are my thoughts on this interesting and fortuitous learning journey that has defined much of my 2021.

Selection Interview

  • Wow, others are much ambitious
  • Why am I here, I don’t even think we can grow much, internationally or locally

Takeaways:

  • Don’t be too shocked when others ask questions that are challenging. They do not have the same understanding of the industry.
  • Try to answer question with confidence without panicking

Orientation

  • “Old” person advice : Get married, settle down because responsibility changes one’s perspectives. 成家立业
  • Be humble, be friendly. Filled only 5% of the conversation.
  • How much effort you put in is how much you get back.
  • Let your children bring their friends home so you can judge their friends.
  • “Boss” mindset is different from “worker” mindset.

Takeaways:

  • I am letting others carry the conversation too much.

Biz Strategy

  • The importance of mission, vision and values.
  • The importance of aligning people to the vision.

Takeaway:

  • Case studies are useful
  • Case studies may not have the full picture as there are some abstract and insiders factors that makes things works
  • Financial ratios are lagging and should not solely determine the company’s performance

Digitalising

  • Have a business perspective when doing data analysis
  • What is the actionable outcome? Will doing the analysis change anything?
  • Use (automated) tools for analysis

Takeaway:

  • Data people will create problems that don’t exist if left alone
  • Some analysis from academia do not make sense

Growing Leadership

  • Inchoate
  • Secret sauce
  • Read the energy. Be consistent and genuine. What you say + what you do + what your body language says = must match

Takeaway:

  • Be your own consultant

Innovation

  • zoom zoom zoom
  • Interesting to hear how others innovate in their industry
  • Innovation S curve

Takeaway:

  • Reputation risks and self-cannibalisation
  • Always ask for feedback

International

  • Different factors when judging distance to other markets
  • Different means to go overseas with differing risks
  • Culture map

Takeaway:

  • Not much theory here
  • A few markets make most of the revenue
  • Accounting and control is difficult

Financing

  • is difficult
  • Debt vs Equity

Takeaway

  • need to revise

People

  • Has changed over the years

Takeaway:

  • Some talents will be prioritised and rewarded more by the company
  • Balance the need to prioritise these talents and the mismatch for others

Report

  • Write well
  • Can’t be done in a day
  • Is a process
  • What is the point of each paragraph
  • Backed by facts
  • High level + detailed enough

Overall, the programme has been tiring, challenging and rewarding. I have grown closer, more confident and less awkward with the others. It has been a respite from my job, but at the same time, burning a lot of my leave and rest time. I have gotten a better understanding of challenges of running a company, the difficulties my boss faces and have forged closer bonds.

Over the months, I have become more comfortable in speaking with others and understanding the group dynamic. From the start where I felt uncomfortable cutting into conversations, to being able to challenge others.

How to think to give feedback:

  • think as an investor
  • find problems/situations where there is weakness in the plan
  • feasibility/value/business viability/usability
  • start with the why
  • why will people pay for it
