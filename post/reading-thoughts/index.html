Books I have read

Published May 30, 2020

I have a folder of notes which I use to record my thoughts on some book I read. Here are a collection of those notes for books I read in 2018 and 2019. Much spoilers, please skip if you intend to read. I don’t agree with everything I wrote back then, but here they are~

Who moved my cheese

Simple simple story of not overthink, feel sorry for yourself, and let emotions cloud your judgment, decisions and actions. Sometimes it is better to be like a mouse and not overthink, don’t let your strengths hold you back. Be aware of your surroundings and don’t be complacent or entitled.

The Leader, The Teacher & You: Leadership Through The Third Generation

Every chapter starts with a short story, similar to the kind that is used in Chinese Comprehension passages back in school, but in English. This story left a deep impression on me. The black belt story - the teacher asks the student what is the meaning of the blackbelt? A recognition of efforts? An assurance of techniques and skills? A testament to the many years of discipline and training? No, the meaning of receiving the blackbelt is not the end, but to be your own shifu and be on a journey with no end to the highest standards beyond yourself.

Another story is about the water and the rock - do you want to be the rock in the stream, gradually become smooth but still by the motion of coursing waters? Or the water that meanders and finds its path no matter what and will not be held back.

As always, the principle is anticipate change, stay relevant.

Disrupt Yourself

Really like this book as it changed my perspective on things.

S-shaped learning curve

  • starting out is always always hard
  • the efforts suddenly pays off and competency increases quickly
  • stagnation is reached where a lot lot lot more effort is needed to reach the very pinnacle
  • recognise the stage and don’t be too demotivated when the results are not proportionate to the effort

Market risk vs Competition risk

  • there is less risk in finding a new market (creating a product for consumers and finding the market) then competitive risk (going into an established market where long time players amassed many resources)
  • look for new opportunities, do not do what others is doing
  • eg Lawyer with social media following, creating a new product, new show, new format

Pivot

  • when necessary make the lateral change
  • don’t be stagnant, change is good, risk is good
  • doing more, recognising how the old skills may be helpful in a new arena and learning the new competencies in the new environment.

In a job, we are hired and get monetary conpensation for our time and effort. In fact, we are also “hiring” the job to fulfill our needs.

  • emotional: to be able to collaborate with others, to mentor and be mentored
  • intellectual: to use our expertise to solve and problem, to be in the right state where the job is challenging but doable
  • social: status from job title, being respected for skills

Small part of the book just talks about how lonely it feels, how lost, no identity, no plan, no idea how to continue.

The Secret Garden

When something is neglected, it withers and dies, but when it is worked on and cared for it thrives. Take care of your garden, everyone~

Leaders eat Last

Circle of security 

  • able to feel safe in circle, job not endangered, no backstabbing, trust and collaboration between members
  • only then can people be fully creative, innovative and give their full potential
  • being outside of this circle makes people stressed, paranoid, unable to focus on their job if they have to guard their job and guard against others
  • there are very real effects from hormones in our bodies. High amounts/ constant cortisol negatively affects our performance in the long run. Dopamine, serotonin encourages/rewards collaboration and helping others.

Millennial

  • entitled
  • coddled
  • looking for a sense of purpose in job
  • resourceful
  • need mentorship/taking care after

How to run a company

  • 150 ppl is the ideal number maximally
  • open communication between decision makers and people closest to the work who have the most information
  • being a leader is not about holding all the power and feeling self important from the power, it is about letting go of this and distributing the power to the people closest to the information/ on the ground who can see and understand the best
  • Rules are meant to be broken as rules work only in a set environment. People do not trust rules, but trust people that know the appropriate time to break these rules.
  • mentorship is good, being resourceful is good, being resilient is good, it takes time to build competency
  • Be clear of the vision of the company to inspire people. Are you working to increase money? What is the product for? Who does it help? In what way does the product spark joy for others?
  • Maximising stockholder’s value is a shitty way to run the company. Stockholders are not the owners of the company. Company should serve the needs of the community/of the consumers.
  • Be kind, be empathetic to others. If a leader, help others/employees. If employee, show concern for leaders too.
  • Leaders eat last, the main message is that leaders serve people, work not for their self interest but for the interest of other. That is the price of power.

Fragile lives

A book about recollections of a cardiac surgeon.

  • Sometimes you win, sometimes you lose. Sometimes the patient dies there nothing you can do.
  • Sometimes you don’t have the best equipment/tools but don’t let that stop you.
  • Start from the very bottom, very humble beginnings, there is nothing beneath you, no ego.
  • Go wherever the opportunities are, be it the other side of the world.
  • Reach out to people, other’s strengths are yours as well.
  • Sometimes the fight does not end with you. Aftercare is important, and sometimes people are too paralyzed to make good decisions and it is not your fault. Lose some win some.
  • Don’t give up.
  • Being in a hospital maybe a normal occurrence for doctors/surgeons but is one of the most important moment for a patient’s life.
  • Do the right thing, don’t take the easy way but know when to stop.
  • At the end, a lot of things are sacrificed, family, friends, person time, time spent with children, all for a job. Some things just have to give way.

Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life

Ikigai - finding your meaning in life, reason for existence doing some you love, you are good at, you can make money from, and contribute to society.

Healthy living - breathing techniques,  yoga,  tachi, moving around often, not seating.

Socialising - being with friends, living

Living in the moment - accepting your emotions, like being in a storm, don’t try to change or reject them, observe them and know that things will change.

Accepting the temporary nature of life and live in the moment. Every experience is unique, special and cannot be repeated.
