Inspired

Published November 5, 2021

I was recommended this book by a supervisor I had. I think of it as a farewell present from the supervisor I worked with for close to 2 years. The first 2 years of work that was not the most smooth sailing, but I have seen my growth that has not come very easily.

The book is kind of like a manual on how to be a product manager, with job descriptions of each member of a product team; tools, methods and processes used by the team.

As someone that has no plans or interest to be a product manager, this was a long read, taking about 8 months. Overall, I am happy to have this informative read and have a little peek into the product management world and workflows in tech companies.

Some points:

  • Don’t make things that people don’t use (learnt this the hard way)
  • Have a vision for the product. Have people buy in to the vision. Convince others by looking at their perspective. Bring the necessary people at the right time. Collaborative process.
  • Start up : new product company that has yet to achieve product/market fit
  • unk unk : unknown unknows
  • Solving problems, not implement features
  • Choose the right MVP and different user research techniques
  • Holistic Product: functionality, technology, UX, monetisation, support
  • Risks : value, usability, feasibility, business viability
  • Use the right processes and techniques

It is a good manual to come back to when creating something for others to use.
